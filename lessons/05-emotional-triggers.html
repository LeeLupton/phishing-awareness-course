Lesson 5: Spotting Emotional Triggers

Why do phishing emails use emotional triggers?

Phishing emails often rely on emotional manipulation to create urgency or fear. Attackers aim to make recipients act impulsively by bypassing logical thinking. Recognizing these triggers can help you identify and avoid phishing attempts.

Common emotional triggers in phishing emails

  • Urgency: Messages claiming "Your account will be deactivated in 24 hours" pressure you to act quickly.
  • Fear: Emails warning of legal action, missed payments, or hacked accounts aim to provoke anxiety.
  • Greed: Promises of unexpected rewards, refunds, or lottery winnings exploit your desire for financial gain.
  • Curiosity: Subject lines like "See this shocking video of you!" encourage you to click without thinking.
  • Authority: Emails pretending to be from executives, government agencies, or IT support exploit trust in authority figures.
