Lesson 1: Introduction to Phishing

What is a phishing email?

A phishing email is a deceptive email that attempts to trick the recipient into sharing sensitive information (e.g., passwords, bank account details) or performing an action that benefits the attacker, like clicking a malicious link.

These emails often appear to be from legitimate organizations, such as banks, service providers, or even trusted coworkers, but their goal is to exploit your trust and steal your information.

What could someone possibly gain from a successful phishing attempt?

A successful phishing attempt can provide the attacker with:

  • Access to your sensitive accounts, such as email or bank accounts.
  • Personal or financial information to commit identity theft.
  • Login credentials to breach company systems.
  • Access to customer information or trade secrets, which can be sold or exploited.

What is the worst that can happen from clicking a random link?

Clicking a malicious link in a phishing email can result in:

  • Malware Installation: The link may download and install harmful software on your device, such as ransomware, spyware, or keyloggers.
  • Credential Theft: The link could lead to a fake login page, where entering your credentials hands them directly to the attacker.
  • System Breach: If connected to a company network, clicking the link could provide attackers with a foothold to compromise the entire system.
