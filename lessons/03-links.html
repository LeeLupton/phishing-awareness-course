Lesson 3: Verifying Links in Emails

Why should you inspect links?

Cybercriminals often disguise malicious links to look legitimate. These links can lead to fake login pages, malware downloads, or other harmful sites. Inspecting links before clicking is crucial to ensure they are safe.

How to inspect links

Here are common ways to check where a link will take you:

  • Hover over the link: Place your cursor over the link to view its destination URL (shown at the bottom of your browser or email client).
  • Right-click and copy: Right-click the link, copy it, and paste it into a text editor to examine its structure.
  • Use link-scanning tools: Websites like VirusTotal or CheckPhish allow you to verify links safely.

Red flags in suspicious links

Watch out for these common red flags in URLs:

  • Misspellings: A legitimate site like https://www.amazon.com may be spoofed as https://www.amaz0n.com (with a "0" instead of "o").
  • Unfamiliar domains: For example, https://paypal-login.support.net is suspicious compared to https://www.paypal.com.
  • Long or complex URLs: Links with excessive subdomains or random characters can indicate phishing attempts (e.g., https://secure-login.paypal.fake-support.com/login.php).

Examples of suspicious and legitimate links

Compare these suspicious links with their legitimate counterparts:

Suspicious Link Legitimate Link
https://www.amaz0n.com https://www.amazon.com
https://secure-paypal.customerservice.com https://www.paypal.com
https://www.bankofamerica-verification.com https://www.bankofamerica.com

What to do if you're unsure

If you’re unsure about a link’s legitimacy, follow these steps:

  1. Do not click the link. Instead, contact the sender directly using a verified phone number or email.
  2. Search for the company’s website in your browser rather than using the link in the email.
  3. Report the suspicious link to your IT team or email provider for investigation.
