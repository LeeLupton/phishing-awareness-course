Lesson 2: Recognizing Suspicious Email Addresses

Why should you check email addresses?

Cybercriminals often disguise their phishing emails to look like legitimate communication. Checking the sender's email address is one of the simplest ways to detect potential phishing attempts. A carefully crafted email might look authentic at first glance but could reveal its true nature upon closer inspection.

Common red flags in email addresses

  • Misspelled domains: Attackers may use addresses like support@rnicrosoft.com instead of support@microsoft.com (notice the "rn" instead of "m").
  • Extra characters: A legitimate address like info@paypal.com might be spoofed as info@paypal-support.com.
  • Generic senders: Be cautious of addresses like noreply@secure.com, which lack context about the organization they represent.
  • Unfamiliar domains: A bank email from support@bankofamerica-customerservice.com should be treated as suspicious.

How to view the full email address

Many email clients hide the sender's full email address by default, showing only the display name (e.g., "Amazon Customer Service"). Here's how you can reveal the full address:

  • Gmail: Click on the sender's name in the email header to expand the full email address.
  • Outlook: Hover over or click on the sender's name to see the email address.
  • Apple Mail: Click the dropdown arrow next to the sender's name to reveal the full address.

Examples of suspicious email addresses

Here are some examples of suspicious and legitimate email addresses:

Suspicious Email Legitimate Email
support@rnicrosoft.com support@microsoft.com
info@paypal-support.com info@paypal.com
customerservice@amazon-secure.net customerservice@amazon.com
