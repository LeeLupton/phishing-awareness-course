Lesson 7: Reporting Suspicious Emails

Why is it important to report suspicious emails?

Reporting phishing emails helps protect your organization and others from cyberattacks. When you report a suspicious email, your IT team or email provider can block the sender, investigate the threat, and prevent further attacks.

How to report phishing emails

Follow these steps to report suspicious emails in popular email clients:

  • Gmail:
    1. Open the suspicious email (do not click any links or download attachments).
    2. Click the three vertical dots in the top-right corner.
    3. Select Report phishing.
  • Outlook:
    1. Right-click the suspicious email in your inbox.
    2. Select Mark as phishing.
    3. Alternatively, forward the email to your IT team or phishing@domain.com.
  • Apple Mail:
    1. Open the email and click the flag icon.
    2. Select Mark as Junk.
    3. Optionally, forward the email to your IT team.

What information should you include when reporting?

When reporting a phishing email, include the following information to help with the investigation:

  • The email headers (if possible).
  • The full senderâ€™s email address.
  • A description of why the email seems suspicious (e.g., unusual links, unexpected attachments).
  • A copy or screenshot of the email, if safe to do so.

What happens after you report a phishing email?

Once you report a phishing email, the following steps may be taken:

  • Your IT team or email provider may block the sender and remove similar emails from other inboxes.
  • They may analyze the email to identify potential threats, such as malware or network breaches.
  • The incident may be shared with cybersecurity organizations or law enforcement to prevent further attacks.

Best practices for reporting phishing emails

  1. Report suspicious emails as soon as possible to minimize risk.
  2. Avoid interacting with the email before reporting (e.g., clicking links or replying).
  3. Encourage colleagues to report similar emails to prevent widespread impact.
