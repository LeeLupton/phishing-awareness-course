Lesson 6: Adding Known Contacts

Why maintain a contact list?

Maintaining a contact list helps you recognize legitimate emails from known senders. Many email clients highlight or prioritize messages from saved contacts, making it easier to spot unfamiliar or suspicious senders.

Attackers often impersonate trusted individuals or organizations. If their email address isn’t in your contact list, it’s a red flag to verify their identity before responding or taking action.

How to add contacts in popular email clients

Here’s how to add contacts in the most commonly used email platforms:

  • Gmail:
    1. Open the email from the sender you want to add.
    2. Click the three vertical dots in the top-right corner of the email.
    3. Select Add to Contacts.
  • Outlook:
    1. Open the email from the sender you want to add.
    2. Right-click on the sender’s name or email address.
    3. Select Add to Outlook Contacts and fill in the details.
  • Apple Mail:
    1. Open the email from the sender you want to add.
    2. Click the sender’s name in the header.
    3. Select Add to Contacts.

Red flags for unknown senders

  • Unfamiliar display names: For example, an email from "Your Bank" with an address like security@unknownbank.com.
  • Emails with no contact history: If you've never interacted with the sender before, verify their identity.
  • Generic greetings: Phrases like "Dear Customer" instead of addressing you by name.

Best practices for managing contacts

  1. Regularly update your contact list to include trusted senders you frequently communicate with.
  2. Delete outdated or inactive contacts to prevent confusion or exploitation by attackers.
  3. Avoid adding contacts from unsolicited or suspicious emails.
