Lesson 8: Recap and Final Tips

Recap of key lessons

  • Lesson 1: Phishing emails are designed to trick you into revealing sensitive information or installing malware. Always verify the sender.
  • Lesson 2: Check email addresses carefully for misspellings, extra characters, or unfamiliar domains.
  • Lesson 3: Inspect links before clicking. Hover over them to check their destination or use link-scanning tools.
  • Lesson 4: Be cautious with attachments, especially unexpected ones. Avoid opening .exe, .zip, or macro-enabled files (.docm, .xlsm).
  • Lesson 5: Recognize emotional triggers like urgency, fear, or greed that phishing emails exploit.
  • Lesson 6: Maintain a contact list to quickly identify legitimate emails from trusted senders.
  • Lesson 7: Report suspicious emails to your IT team or email provider to help protect your organization and others.

Final tips for staying safe

  1. Stay skeptical: Treat any unexpected email with caution, especially if it asks for sensitive information or urges immediate action.
  2. Keep software updated: Ensure your email client, browser, and antivirus software are up to date to reduce vulnerability to attacks.
  3. Double-check URLs: Manually type known URLs into your browser instead of clicking links in emails.
  4. Enable two-factor authentication: Protect your accounts by requiring a second form of verification during login.
